Cricket: A Three Format Game

Cricket has three formats which amplifies the sport as one of the most unique sports in the world. The three formats are:

  • Test (First Class)
    • First Class matches range from 3-5 days in length.
    • Test matches (international version of first class) last 5 days, unless a decisive result is reached earlier.
    • Days last around 8 hours with three short breaks throughout the day.
    • Days last 90 overs.
    • Each team has two innings.
    • To win, a team must bowl their oponents out completely in both innings, and successfully defend their accumulated total or successfully chase their opponents set target.
  • One Day Internationals (List A)
    • List A matches last roughly 10 hours.
    • Each team has one innings.
    • Each team has 50 allotted overs.
    • One Day International is the international version of List A cricket.
    • This format has a World Cup event hosted every four years.
    • To win, a team must restrict their oponents to a total less than the team or successfully chase the total set by the opposition.
  • T20 Cricket
    • Matches last roughly 4 hours.
    • Each team has one innings.
    • Each team has an allotted 20 overs.
    • T20 Cricket has a World Cup event hosted every four years.
    • To win, a team must restrict their oponents to a total less than the team or successfully chase the total set by the opposition.

Cricket: The Future Holds Hope

Cricket is not a very popular sport in America. Yet we can hope it becomes as mainstream as possible. Here are some reasons why interest in the sport is rising fast:

  • With New York City as the melting pot that it is, the continuous-growing diverse population is allowing for more people from around the world to share their love of the sport.
  • The rise of the shortest format, T20 Cricket, is attracting many younger fan from non-traditional cricketing nations.
  • Cricket may be on the Summer Olympics very soon.
  • The partial resemblance to baseball will be a key factor in capturing the interest of many baseball fans.
